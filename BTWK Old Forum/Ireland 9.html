  3. JasperClay - 24-02-2015, 21:23
    EDIT: Edited for clarity (I hope)

    1.) Okay, awesome, forget what I said. Unique and fun is better than copy-paste.

    2.) It definitely is a large Dal Riata. I'm just trying to think from a player perspective. If I'm a Dal Riatan ruler who's powerful enough to form Alba, why are my ancestral lands in Ulaid not de jure part of my Kingdom, even if I hold them? I mean, at that point, I'd probably have a claim to all of Ulster - I'm definitely at least as powerful as the O'Neills. Frankly, if there was a Pictland thread, I'd propose the following there:

    i.) The Pictish Kingdoms in modern Scotland are under a de jure Empire that the Romano-British would call the "High Kingdom of Caledonia". That's essentially the base de jure now, I think, though I don't know what the Romano-British localization is.

    ii.) Dal Riata is without a de jure Empire. I'm fairly certain that's okay - I think SWMH does that all the time in HIP. If it has to be under Caledonia, so be it.

    The following are then possible unified High Kingdoms:

    a.) The (High) Kingdom of Alba can be formed by a ruler who holds The (High) Kingdom of Caledonia and either The Kingdom of Strathclyde or The Kingdom of Dal Riata.

    b.) If a Non-Pict Ruler of Dal Riata also holds Ulster (or, Dal Riata also holds 1.) The Kingdom of (Strathclyde and/or Galloway) and 2.) The Kingdom of Man) then it can form the High Kingdom of Scottia(/The Isles/The Gaels.)

    c.) A ruler who holds The High Kingdom of Scottia(/The Isles/The Gaels) and The High Kingdom of Caledonia can form the High Kingdom of The Gaels(/Scottia/Gaelica) which would be essentially Alba + Strathclyde + Galloway + Man + Ulster.

    Now, we have a very fun Ireland + Scotland. So, if you become High King of Ireland (and Dal Riata, I suppose), and want to make those Picts into Scots with your angry Hibernian armies, you have a logical way to trade the integrity of your de jure empire for claims to titles on Albion. Want to make the Scottish-Irish dual monarchy that the most ambitious kings in both countries have thought about? Go for it, we've provided a way to redraw the map for fun, profit, and playtime. Are you a super-powerful Dal Riatan ruler, who holds lands on both sides of the Irish Sea? Rock and Roll! See if you can create a state not even the Vikings could hold together. Are you a Pict who's brought together all of Caledonia? Well, become a Scotsman, and reverse the spread into Ireland!

    Balancing the steamrollers is 3rd on my list, after getting traits integrated into history, and putting in those buildings. But players want to become steamrollers - right now, when they do, there's not only no challenges, there are no goals. I'll stay out of England, because you guys seem to have a lot of plans there. But to make Alba, Ireland, or bust the only results for Irish/Pict interaction, I think, overestimates the inevitability of the formation of Alba/Scotland as we know it. To be fair, I might be wildly wrong. But I think it's a fun wrong.

    3.) Yeah, DC, you (and Ols) are right. Breakaway, revolt, and title-destorying Gavelkind are better solutions than Titular Kingdoms everywhere.
