  6. BlackEagle78 - 28-02-2016, 17:55
    Oh right, maybe you could add events to simulate how the Alemanni got to Swaffham? I was thinking of this:
    -If the province of Swaffham is non-Ingvaeonic and non-Alemannic, and ruled by a lord who isn't of these cultures, and if a continental (!) Ingvaeone is fighting a war for it (or a war which would result in incorporation of the province if won), the Ingvaeone would get an event that a large band of Alemannic soldiers has gathered outside of his capital, and that they offer him their services in return for a fertile land to settle. If the Ingvaeone accepts the offer, an army of 1000-1500 warriors spawns in his capital and becomes his to use in the war. If he refuses, the angry Alemanni either return home or rise up in revolt to gain a county of the Ingvaeone himself.
    -If the Ingvaeone wins the war, he gets another event giving him the choice between giving Swaffham to the leader of the warband (granting him the county and making him a vassal of the Ingvaeone), or refusing to do so. Refusing to do so will have the warband declare an adventurer war for Swaffham against the Ingvaeone, and will lower the latters prestige by a great deal.
    -If the Ingvaeone loses the war, the Alemanni will become a mercenary group available for hiring for lords in Britain.
    -A few months after the province is taken and granted to the leader of the Alemanni, another event will have the army ship over its family to the newly conquered land, and will give Swaffham Alemannic culture.

    A similar mechanic could be applied to the Frisians in Britain, both in Kent and around the Firth of Forth (where I read a lot of Frisians also settled).
  7. BlackEagle78 - 28-02-2016, 18:07
    Another thing you could add if you have spare time is a burial mechanic for Pagan lords. If a friend or close kin dies, the lord gets an event which makes him able to choose in which way to bury the deceased. The cheapest way would be to cremate them or bury them in a normal grave; this would be free and would have no consequences. The second way would be to build a small burial hall and shrine; this would cost a little bit of gold, and would grant some prestige. The third, and most expensive option would be to build a burial hill for the deceased; this would cost a lot of money, but would give a lot of prestige and a modifier named "honours the dead", which gives members of his religion a small opinion bonus (which obviously doesn't stack).
    For Ingvaeones and North-Germanics, there would be a fourth option: a ship burial. This would also cost a significant amount of gold, but would grant a similar amount of prestige as the hill burial, and also gives the same modifier.

    The second, third, and fourth options would also give a small opinion bonus concerning close family members and friends of the deceased.
    Also, to show that with royalty comes responsibility, I suggest that Pagan kings and emperors would lose prestige with the first option; people would look dishonourably upon a high lord who just "dumps" the body of a friend or member of the close kin, instead of giving them a respectable burial.

    I think that this event would both be realistic and interesting from a role-playing perspective.
