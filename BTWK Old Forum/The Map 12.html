    With succession, I actually mean kings being murdered/deposed, and being replaced by non-dynasts. I wont do this especially frequently, but it in places where we don't have historical accounts it seem sensible for weak kings to be shown as deposed. I've created a 'usurper' element in the history of a few titles already (in Broceliande for example). By creating links between cousins and by having aggrieved vassals preset in history we make it so that the tribes are not annoyingly stable for the first few years.   10. DC123456789 - 12-07-2015, 19:14
    Yeah - it's for cities, army positions, councilor positions, and ports (if applicable). Here's how to do it:

    You can activate the nudge tool by making nudge_allowed into yes in settings.txt; you'll see a new Nudge button in the main menu. When you go there you'll be able to left click on provinces to select them, and then right click to put in the various positions (only councillor, city, army, and port for ports are relevant). There will be a menu to let you choose between which position you're changing, as well as make minute adjustments for x, y, and z as well as the rotating (mostly for ports). It's pretty intuitive and simple to use, really. The sea provinces also need positions, they act even more strangely than land provinces if they aren't there.
    And for saving/committing your positions:

    It overrides the vanilla positions.txt at Program Files (x86)\Steam\SteamApps\common\Crusader Kings II\map. Just copy the file over to the Britannia map folder (and remember to verify the integrity of your game cache when you want to play vanilla again).
    I believe Ols has already done Dumnonia, Kernow, Stronggore, Cantia, and Anglia, but all the other provinces still need to be done.
