  2. DC123456789 - 28-05-2015, 18:01
    I don't really think Briton needs to be subdivided, it's not terribly big as is anyway. Maybe a future Welsh/Cornish development (if applicable - not sure), but I don't really see that as much of a priority.
    Alemanni will definitely make it in soon (probably to be distinct from Suebi for gameplay reasons).
    I don't think there's much of a point splitting Frankish either.
    The Huns I think we were thinking of making Altaic and Tengri pagan. Doesn't really matter what we call it though given they'll be in their own culture group anyway.
    Graeco-Roman isn't really a melting pot per se, it basically just represents Greek.
    Norman definitely seems out of timeframe, but I think we should have French, Occitan and maybe Arpitan as Romano-Gallic + Frankish melting pots.
    Not sure if Norse-Gaels are particularly in the time frame, but if they are I'm open to it.
    Edit
  3. DC123456789 - 28-05-2015, 19:09
    Basques/Vascones will definitely make it more southwest France/northeast Iberia. Not sure about the rest of Southern France, but I think just making the rest of it Romano-Gallic would work. WtWSMS's setup for Hispania would work - most of it being Romano-Hispanic, with some settled Visigoths, Suebi, Alans, and Vandals (the latter two primarily for 410, though maybe small minorities remaining by 479), and some reamining Celtiberians (Gallaeci, Astures and Cantabri - distinct cultures or not?) in the north.

  5. DC123456789 - 29-05-2015, 07:55
    I don't think we need a Gallic culture; Ols and I basically decided that most of the Gauls were assimilated enough to be considered Romano-Gallic, and any truly Celtic remnants would basically just fall under Armorican (which itself, like the Britons and Cumbrics, are significantly Romanized anyway, just not to the degree of the Romano-Britons/Gauls).
    Breton will definitely make it as a pseudo-melting pot (really more event-driven) of Briton and Armorican once we get around to that.
    Celtiberians should probably go under the Celtic umbrella.
    I haven't really thought much about Italy but I expect it would probably be thoroughly Roman at this point. Not sure if there's a point breaking up the Imperial culture group though.
    What were you thinking of for Latin? Proto-Italian?
    I was also thinking that concurrently to the French/Occitan/Arpitan melting pot Frankish should also break into Dutch and Franconian.

  6. Isinfier - 29-05-2015, 08:48
    Gallic was more of a placeholder, assuming that the timeline is extended either way. I'll shift Breton over to melting pot.     I'll throw up Dutch and Franconian as well, as well as Bavarian.

    The map projection is the same as the War of the Roses, aye?
  7. Frednutts - 29-05-2015, 09:33
    For Italy, I'd suggest simply splitting the peninsula into Roman and Italian - Roman around Rome, Italian (or perhaps Italic, to differentiate from the Lombard Italian) most other places, then perhaps Greek/Sicilian in the south and in Sicily. If you wanted to divide it more, you could put the northern bit as Tuscan/Etruscan and the southern but as Neapolitan. Sardinian could definitely be its own culture, but Corsica would probably share it.

    Continental celts should probably be in a separate group to insular celts, yes.
  8. DC123456789 - 29-05-2015, 16:51
    I don't think we'll ever have a need for Gallic - 410 is about as early as we're planning to go. Splitting off the Celtiberians into their own group could work, but remember that they were still related enough for migrating Britons to decide to take refuge there. I do wish CK2 had a better culture grouping system, the big Germanic group could possibly be subdivided as well.

    Southern Italy, including probably all of Sicily will definitely be Greek; I still don't think that there was really enough of a distinction among the other Romans of Italy to merit splitting Roman, though. Remember that by this point all of Italy would have been under Roman rule for over 600 years, with a common Latinized culture stretching over all of the WRE. Etruscans had been gone for 300 years at best from 410 and Naples was in fact a Greek city IIRC well into the Middle Ages. Not entirely sure that Sardinia merits its own culture at start either (of course, this would all change after the Lombards invade...).
    Edit
  9. Frednutts - 29-05-2015, 17:11
    I still think an Italian culture, separate from Roman, would be preferable. It really depends on what we mean by 'culture'. Legally speaking, every free man in the empire was 'Roman', but culturally speaking, though 'Romanisation' happened, the Romans were happy to let locals keep their own dialect, traditions etc, even on as close a proximity as Italy.
    I only suggest Sardinian because the Romans only ever kept to the shores of the island. The local native culture was never assimilated or integrated. Most of the inner parts of the island were never touched by the Romans.
