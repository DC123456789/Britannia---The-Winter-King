  1. Ols - 24-11-2014, 08:39
    Looking in to it more carefully, it looks like our best precedent would be for Rhegin, Celemion and the Belgae as one kingdom. Historically, the Belgae were the junior partner of a confederation with the Atrebates (Celemion). Ceint could be something on its own, given its apparent strength during the Roman withdrawal. Ceint could also pair with Londinium into a Kingdom of some kind.

    It is mentioned that Cynwidion may have absorbed Lundein for a very brief period. We could show this as happening in the history as well. In which case, we generally have no choice other than making Ceint its own kingdom.

    Overall, my research suggests you're right about lumping Belgae and Celemion together - I'm not sure whether we should demote Gwintguic to a duchy or let them have the title though - I get the impression that Celemion dominated.

    Putting Rhegin, Celemion, and the Belgae together (as Atrebatia?) seems fine. I would probably leave them all as duchies, though, so Dumnonia has a chance to eventually subjugate them to reach its extent in 479.

  10. Frednutts - 25-11-2014, 12:05
    Once the Ireland overhaul is done, we still need to work on Wales and the west country for the 410 start. I know you've been using this (450 - 600) map for the sub-Roman kingdoms, but this one (400 -425) gives a more appropriate version of Wales and the west country for the 410 start. It has the correct layouts for Magnus Maximus' reorganised coastline, with his sons ruling North Wales (until he got replaced by Cunedag) (Gwynedd), South Wales (Demetia) and Mid-South Wales (Cernyw).
    We need to re-jig the map around south Wales to allow proper room for Builth, Brycheiniog, and Gwent.
    Vortigern should start in 410 as an independent, ducal leader in the Powys region, in a position to become king of Powys (should be called Pagenses then) as he did historically in c.418. His marriage to Severa Macsen should ally him with Magnus' other descendants who rule South and Mid-South Wales at the time. This should put him in a position to mount a bid for the high kingship by c.425 as he did historically. His realm at that point should cover Powys, Gwent, and Builth & Gwerthrynion (one kingdom). On becoming High King, he devolves his lands to his sons. Vortimer gains Gwent, Cadeyrn gains Pagenses, and Pasgen gains Builth & Gwerthrynion.
    Then we need to sort out the timeline for the war between Ambrosius Aurelianus and Vortigern, and what happens to what lands after that.
