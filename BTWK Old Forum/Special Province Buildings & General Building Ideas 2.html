  8. JasperClay - 05-03-2015, 00:58
    Okay, so I've done some math on buildings, with the idea that i'll code a whole overhaul this weekend. But I'd like feedback, first - it'll be a significant overhaul. Also, I'm tired, so I can't work this out any better at the moment, but I want to start coding this weekend.

    I.) First, levy buildings.
    1.)They're actually not too badly balanced with mercs, frankly. I was thinking of slashing the levies provided by all of them in half, as part of the great levy reduction. Then, the cost of these buildings would be linear. Each level gets you X troops, which costs (2-4)*(troop value)*(# of troops). So, for archers, HI, and Pikemen, which all have a troop value of 2 (in defines), a building that provides 25 more would cost (2-4)*(2)*(25) = 100-200 gold. This cost would not increase with building level, and you get the attack/defense bonus for free. It's pretty close to the current cost of the trees, I think, in their entirety. We can play with that (2-4) constant to make it feel right. It's hard to value levies vs. mercs - maybe the constant should be 2. Since players will also be building population, we might lower costs to make this a bit less onerous.
    2.) The build time on levy buildings would be flatly doubled (or maybe *1.5). So, the higher-level buildings wouldn't cost more, but they'd take even more time.
    3.) I'd like to remove +% levies from all buildings, and replace 5% with something like +50 retinue cap, as per the discussion on the balance thread.

    II.) Econ/Special buildings
    1.) Farms and the like will be set to pay themselves off in a generation. (20-30 years). They'll probably take ~10 years to build. I've already worked this out, somewhere, and I promise it's not unreasonable.
    2.) Population will cost a flat 50 gold, but will take variable years to build per level (not strictly increasing.) This will come in when the destruction events are coded, I think. We can talk more about it, then. Provinces will location-locked to certain maximum buildable population levels, though. Most will be limited to normal (5), but I'd say The Fens should be lock to 3, The Pennies to 4, Coastal/Major river provinces to 6, and major cities to 7, for example.
    3.) I think the costs/build times of my 'infrastructure buildings' will remain as they are. I might reduce some of their bonuses and lower costs, but that's probably what will happen when population comes in.

    III.) Holdings
    I'd like to make castles, cities, and temples have the same build cost, but take 10, 20, and 15 years to build, respectively. I think I'd also like to normalize their troop bonuses to 50 Pikes for a castle, 50 militia for a city, and 25LI/25P for a temple. I'm not sure what the cost should be, yet, though I'd like it to be flat, regardless of slot number. I'm thinking something like 150-250 gold (150 works, if we're throwing in the troops for free.)

    I'm just looking for general feedback on how expensive troops should be, if we feel okay about holdings having equal cost, and anything in between. If anyone comes up with a way to equate X yearly gold income/total gold cost to Y levies to Z mercs (to W retinues) let me know. Otherwise, we'll have to guess and check. The idea is to make sure you're always building things all over your demesne, if possible, but to have those things determined by your wealth and needs.
