  1. DC123456789 - 28-01-2016, 14:23
    For the empire proper, I was thinking of this:
    - Roman
    - Romano-British
    - Romano-Gallic
    - Romano-Germanic
    - Romano-Hispanic
    - Romano-Rhaetian
    - Romano-African
    - "Romano-Annonarian" (Cisalpine Gaul, i.e. Northern Italy)
    - Romano-Sardinian
    - Graeco-Roman
    - Romano-Illyrian(?)
    - Romano-Pannonian(?)

    Potentially, we could also have a Romano-Aquitanian culture and split Romano-Hispanic between Romanized Iberians and Romanized Celtiberians, but I'm not sure how much that would make sense or what it would add.

    Picts and Irish could assimilate into Romano-British and West Germanic cultures into Romano-Germanic, and any invaders would end up assimilating into the local culture (for example, conquered Alans in Gaul would end up Romano-Gallic, and conquered Visigoths in Hispania would become Romano-Hispanic. Then the only other Roman melting pot cultures we'd really need to whole map are a "Romano-Scandian" culture for the Norse and "Romano-Slavic" culture for any Slavs.

  2. Ols - 28-01-2016, 14:52
    The changes I'd make would be:

    Romano-Sardinian --> Sardinian.
    Graeco-Roman --> Italiot or Griko (include a standard Greek culture as well possibly).
    Don't introduce Romano-Pannonian (I don't anticipate us adding Pannonia given the shape of the map).
    Romano-Rhaetian ---> Rhaetian (possibly, since we wont include the ancient Rhaetians).
    Unsure about Romano-Annonarian, it depends where Roman culture itself is made to extend to, as the south shouldn't be all Roman either.
    I agree with Romano-Aquitanian for Basques, I don't think they should merge given their distinct culture.
    Romano-Celtiberian isn't needed though, since the foundations of Spanish culture would have been laid already by this time.
    Romano-Slavic could be included, not sure what we're going to do for Slavic invasions.
    I'm sympathetic to Romano-Gaelic, since I'm not sure they'd be identical to the Brits and it'd be an interesting reward for someone who conquers out that far to get a new culture (with some peculiarities maybe).
    Another one could be Romano-Semitic as a sort of easter egg, representing Jews and Arabs under Roman rule. We could add this some way into the future if we add the Jewish diaspora and Arab invasions. I think this could be one exception to the sensible rule that minor nomadic cultures should simply take the local Romanised culture as they settle. Allowing for a Jewish state (of which one existed in the timeline in Morocco) to take on a unique culture would be nice. Another option is making a distinction between Neo-Punic as "Romano-Semitic" and Berber as Romano-African.

  5. DC123456789 - 28-01-2016, 15:20
    I'd prefer to keep the Romano- prefix to emphasize that it's a Romanized culture. We already have Romano-Gallic without a corresponding Gallic culture, after all.
    I don't think Magna Graecia merits a separate Greek culture from Greece proper. Griko and Italoit are modern names anyway and seem anachronistic as names.
    I think Aquincum/Budapest (and therefore the rest of the bend of the Danube) is just on the map actually. It could be useful for representing the pre-conquest of Italy Ostrogoths. Note that Dalmatia was under Roman, then Odacer's control until the Ostrogoths took over all of Italy.
    I think it was you (Ols) who proposed Romano-Annonarian earlier actually, to differentiate what would become the Gallo-Italic languages from the rest of the Italians. Doesn't really matter to me all that much.
    Romano-Gaelic (I'd prefer Romano-Hibernian or Romano-Scotian as a name) would work, I was just being conservative about "hypothetical" Roman melting pots.
    I considered splitting Romano-African into Romanized Punic and Romanized Numidian/Berber branches, but there was a lot of mixture between the groups in Roman Africa so I'm not sure if it would make all that much sense. I don't think a Romanized Jewish or Romanized Arab culture is really needed since they never formed a majority anywhere and would most likely just assimilate into the local culture if conquered by Romans, much like what the Alans, Burgundians, etc. ended up doing.
