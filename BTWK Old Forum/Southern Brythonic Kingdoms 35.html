    I've stopped making changes today, I'll come back and reconsider tomorrow. Chilternsaete will be done properly tomorrow. Meonware isn't "re-established" as such - I'm debating whether to give the Belgae title to Cerdic to trigger the de jure change. He doesn't hold Venta, which is fairly critical. My consideration for Gwinttguic and Celemion is that there would have been some kind of stalemate with Meurig - he couldn't establish control, so he accepted their de facto leaders on the condition they become Christian. For simplicity's sake, it all happens on the same day Sagramor dies. I can't find any tradition of the Meonware actually being independent - it looks like they're a cultural group rather than a kingship title.

    This is what I took into consideration for Wight:
    The island is apparently ruled directly by Cerdic, king of the West Seaxe (according to their later records). Wihtgar (if he exists) is installed as a client king in 534, probably upon Cerdic's death. The Jutes of Wight retain probable family links to the royal house of the West Seaxe (Alfred the Great's mother descends from them), although these are more likely established after the 686 invasion.
    I figured I might as well make a tribute Cerdic, one that isn't obviously the same person.
