  1. Frednutts - 18-11-2014, 20:56
    In the same way that Magnus Maxiums appointed Coel Hen to govern the northernmost region of Britannia to defend against Pictish incursions, he also appointed his sons to govern the westernmost territories against Welsh incursions. Some of the sons are already in the game, but with inaccuracies, and others are omitted entirely.

    Some points:

    - The current 410 ruler of Gwynedd, Cunedag, should not be the son of Custennin. Custennin should be the son of Magnus Maximus and Elen Lwyddog , and should be Romano-British, while Cunedag should be a Pictish native who rules after Custennin's death in 390.
    - The current 410 ruler of Dyfed should be renamed Anwn, and should be a son of Magnus.
    - The current 410 ruler of Cernyw, Owain Finddu, should also be the son of Magnus.
    - Magnus had two other sons, Victor (born c.354) and Peblig (born c.364), and two daughters, Gratianna (born c.367) and Severa (born c.370) who marries Vortigern (who should also be born c.370).
    - Magnus should be alive 335 - August 28, 388 and perhaps should be named Macsen (his Welsh name, which would make his children ap/ferch Macsen, as they were historically). Magnus should be pure Roman culture and Nicene religion, and Peblig should also be Nicene (as he was made a St.). His wife should be Elen Lywddog, a Romano-British Nicene.

    Though Magnus was done and dusted by the 410 start date, his children were very much around and involved in British politics, one of whom (Owain Finddu) holding onto his territory until c.440. I would add them in myself, and may do if no-one else does, but I thought I'd run it past y'all first. It affects Cornwell-regions, but this is all many years before the books begin, so doesn't break our rule of giving Cornwell precedence.

    I've fixed the Clotri issue.

    I'm also eager to get Vortigern and his descendants right - his claim to the High Kingship was mostly validated by his marriage to Magnus Maximus' daughter (can't remember which one offhand). Like Coel Hen and Magnus Maximus, he too, upon his achieving the High Kingship, distributed his former lands amongst his heirs, creating a series of authoritative regions that eventually became celtic-style kingdoms. These include Gwerthefyriwg (Gwent), Pagenses (Powys), and Builth. Many of these are still ruled by his descendants in the 479 start.
    I think Vortigern should be one of the central characters in the 410 start, as he is made High King only 15 years later after his gaining the Kingdom of Dumnonia. It would however require the redrawing of the map in Wales. Ignoring the Roman provinces, this map gives a good idea of the layout of the welsh regions at the beginning of the 5th century.
