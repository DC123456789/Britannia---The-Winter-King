  1. Ols - 18-02-2015, 10:05
    I think we're moving towards filling history and titles with lists of ancestors stretching back to 1 AD. I like that we're doing this, but let's now keep track of these lists in one place so we know where we're at. So far, I've done Ireland and Pictavia back to the year 1. Frednutts has done (or has almost done) the Western Roman Empire. Here are a few more we could do:

    1) High Kings of Britain (non Roman Emperor ones)
    2) The Gallic Empire (any usurper who held a broadly similar title could be included provided they don't overlap)
    3) The Merovingian mythos (descent from Jesus as an easter egg?)
    4) The line of Calcagus (who resisted the Romans in Pictavia and has a blood trait)
    5) The Kings of Angeln, Jutland and Saxony (off screen for now)
    6) Frisian kings/warlords
    7) Gothic peoples (Visigoths, Burgundians)
    8) Each Irish kingdom (actually fairly high priority in my opinion)
    9) The Papacy (because if we extent the map we have strong grounds to get rid of the "Papal Nuncio" concept)
    10) Briton warlords of the Roman conquest era (c. 50 AD) and their descendants

    We don't need to do all of them, but I like the idea of history being expansive and engaging, making distant relatives out of most of the characters. In many cases I think we should go down the mythical route and include people even if they're probably an invention of history (because it's entertaining).
