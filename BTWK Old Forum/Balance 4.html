Buildings have their advantages, but even then, it would be really annoying to put them in every holding in a province, so it would probably be province capitals only.

  5. JasperClay - 03-03-2015, 18:21
    Yeah, I agree with those sentiments, Ols. I just don't know how hard set up would be with province modifiers - maybe DC has a better idea? That being said, I'll be putting up link to a table, shortly, with my ideas on population for the 410/479 start dates. I figure we could go by de jure kingdom, then by duchy for certain areas, and specifically by province for things like London.

    The other thing is, I'd like to pseudo-location lock certain population levels. (Pseudo-lock because events would take precedence). Some of this could be done with the "borders_major_river" and "is_coast" conditions being preconditions for organically getting the higher population levels, and particularly poor terrain locking you out of "normal" population. Other parts of it will have to be done by province id, I think.

    I'd essentially expect that, in most cases, the modifiers would decay from extreme to more normal levels over time. I'd expect that this would be done by event, but if that's too intensive, we could put timers on them, and just have the disappearance of one modifier trigger the reappearance of another. We could use a combination of buildings and modifiers - the building would do nothing but trigger an event that applies the modifier, and events and on_actions (looting, sieges) would change the building, which would then trigger a new modifier.

  8. Ols - 03-03-2015, 19:48
    We also have the principle of "cultural decay" which I imagine could be implemented eventually. Basically, lack of contact with Roman culture or poor maintenance of infrastructure or cultural practices should allow a feudal culture to downgrade into a tribal one - Dumnonia could become mostly Briton. If we tied that into balance, a really bad ruler who ends up with low density population and broken settlements could wind up Briton/Cumbric rather than Romano-Briton. This doesn't strictly tie in, but it's something to ponder if we want another variable in settlement growth/decline. I also considered that if we have Angle/Saxon/Jute and an Anglo-Saxon melting pot, we could make it so that all Angles, Saxons and Jutes are tribal and all Anglo-Saxons are feudal. The growth from one culture to the next should entail administrative change.

  10. JasperClay - 03-03-2015, 20:16
    This might be a terrible solution to buildings v. modifiers, but what if we did the following?

    Population is a modifier - but the modifier is applied based on the status of a building. So, buildings can be used for their convenience, but provinces will display their modifiers for the aesthetics. When the building changes, that will trigger events that change the population modifier. Things that effect the modifiers could also change the buildings - allowing for a simultaneous organic growth and decay, even as the building side is player-driven. The buildings would be dummies, effectly, granting something like 0.01 yearly income.

    This would also allow us to have population change on the shift from castle->tribal, or vis-versa, because of the buildings transferring. It would allow the buildings to be downgraded easily by war, but also allow us to put timers on the more extreme modifiers.
