  7. BlackEagle78 - 09-11-2015, 16:23
    Hey guys,
    I've been thinking about the setup of the region of Larger Frisia for a while, and I think it can be improved a bit. With the conclusion reached in the thread about the British coastline, namely:
    1. Areas that were covered in fens or marshes are portrayed as land provinces, and
    2. These areas have a province modifier which devalues the lands a bit, and
    3. These areas can eventually be drained, which would remove this modifier,
    an idea for Frisia popped up in my head: the areas of the Wadden Sea which lie dry at ebb should work the same way. After reading up on the region some more, I found out that North Frisia as it is today (and in the mod) is the result of centuries of overflowing by the sea. I then decided to modify the entire area from the northern tip of Jutland upto the straits of Calais, to make it more historically plausible and more interesting.



    Land areas that have been added (added provinces are in bold. On the map, they are colored in black or grey):
    • Northern Jutland. Here, Vestervig was added because of reasons explained at the water-section.
    • North Frisia. Added here are (from north to south): Fanø, Röm, Sylt, Föra, North Strand and South Strand. Nowadays, these are islands, but in the Early Middle Ages, they were still mostly attached to the mainland.
    • Helgoland. This province was added after I read that it used to be much larger, and included large marshes around the islets that remain nowadays.
    • The Wadden Coast. As I said before, I made the areas which empty during ebb to land, and they should get the marsh\fen modifier as well.
    • Texel. Meant to represent the historical area of Texla, which used to be connected to North-Holland.

    Water areas that have been added (added water provinces are in bold. On the map, changes in the water areas are colored in white):
    • Limfjord. I read that it used to be open untill the Late Middle Ages, but the western entrance was some kilometres to the south. If the area north of the old entrance belonged to Þjóðland, it would be too big, so I split Þjóðland in half and made the southern half the new province of Vestervig. The Limfjord was sailable during this period.
    • The Nissum Fjord.
    • The Ringkobing Fjord.
    • The Vlie Strait. This narrow strait connected the North Sea to Lake Flevo.
    • Lagunes of Flanders. There were several continuously flooded areas behind the initial line of dunes along what is the coastline nowadays. Part of the Litus Saxonicum.
    I hope I made a good case. It may not be a suprise that I am from the Netherlands myself, and I hope that we can reach a good end result in this land from which my ancestors come. I am happy to discuss further changes.

  10. DC123456789 - 09-11-2015, 19:17
    Ah, I see. I don't really have anything better, but something does like a bit off about the North Frisian provinces. Maybe make the straits look a little more natural? I don't know, but Frisia proper and East Frisia look natural while North Frisia doesn't for whatever reason. I'm probably just prejudiced against overly-stylized, inaccurate medieval maps.

    I'm not that experienced with modding the physical map either, but I've dabbled in it a bit and I can probably explain it enough to get you going.
    - map/provinces,bmp - you've probably already figured this one out, that's where all the provinces in the game are mapped out. Each province needs its own unique colour but otherwise can be anything; by convention water provinces are various shades of blue (obviously) and land provinces in the same region/duchy/kingdom are similar colours, preferably also to their ingame counterparts (but we don't really care much about that).
    - map/rivers.bmp - where all the rivers are mapped out. For your purposes it doesn't really matter much, but all sea provinces (including navigable rivers) are pink, land is white, rivers (not navigable ones) are blue, river sources are green, and where two (non-navigable) rivers meet are red. The colours are indexed so don't change them; all you really need to do here is make all the sea pixels pink.
    - map/terrain.bmp - where the terrain for each pixel is mapped out. It doesn't have to be directly related to the terrain of the provinces; IIRC if a terrain is defined in the province history that is the one used regardless of anything else, and if none is defined it defaults to the dominant terrain in terrain.bmp. To be honest I'm not entirely sure what each colour actually means, but it looks like the light green might be plains, red farmlands, and dark green marsh. Maybe. What's important is that sea zones are blue; if you just copy the general pattern from neighbouring areas for the new land you'll probably be fine.
    - map/topology.bmp - where the height of map is mapped out. It's greyscale, and the whiter the colour, the higher it is. Given that these are very low-lying areas it would probably make sense just to colour in all your new land with the lowest possible land height (compare the map to other maps to find it - a lot of the Netherlands is very low), and vice versa for the shallow sea zones you're adding. You might also have to adjust the seabed heights to make more sense for where the land juts out more than currently.
    - map/trees.bmp - where the trees are mapped out. You can probably safely ignore this.
    - map/word_normal_height.bmp - maps out the lighting or something like that. It's generated from topology.bmp, and probably just be ignored for now. It requires Photoshop IIRC, I just asked mjohnson to help do it for us last time.
    - map/terrain/colormap.bmp - as far as I can tell, it's a blurred satellite image and probably plays a major part in how the map looks. Water is just the plain blue, but I don't really have much advice on how to fill in the land other than just to fudge together colours to make it look kind of like the rest of it.
    - map/terrain/colormap_water.bmp - not entirely sure what this does, but land is just the plain light blue and sea is the dark blue. Just change it as you change the coastline.

    Some words of warning: Always check off "Do not write color space information" when exporting (always use "Export As" when exporting for the first time), make sure the encoding is saved as 24 bits, R8 G8 B8 (NOT A8 R8 G8 B8), try to copy and paste colours when possible for the various physical/terrain maps, and always be sure to set the threshold for finding similar colours to 0. The CK2 wiki page is quite helpful.
