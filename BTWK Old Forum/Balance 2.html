  10. Immortal Impi - 27-11-2014, 16:57
    http://i.imgur.com/9hqMPLO.jpg?1

    Woo! I'm in. Above is the map relevent to the below; it's an observer run from 410-473. I wanted to go to 479 but it was getting late and wanted to post results.

    Some notes.

    -My suggestion for Hen Ogledd is to have an event after Coen Hen's death and break up the empire, destroying the title and splitting them into their constituents. I'm not sure about the actual history of the united kingdom, but certainly if we want to reach somewhat analogous kingdoms in 479 from the 410 start that sort of event might be good. Maybe there should be an option for players to as the son have a choice; either refuse to surrender the title ("Hen Ogledd will not die so long as I live!") or surrender it and have it be destroyed, maybe with the former resulting in a war? Don't know there. Just shooting out suggestions; it has not yet broken up and the independent kingdoms that form when a king dies tend to be forced back in via de jure wars.
    -Dumnonia has already been covered. They conquered the Jutes which is a bit of an issue. Important to note the Jutes were expanding quite prodigiously into Cantware because of Dumnonia's wars there until they were the last ones left and Dumnonia turned on them. Maybe in the case they are annexed early on (this would be after Dumnonia's de jure is cut down to prevent them from being huge) there can be an event which frees the province and starts an auto-independence war for them with a large stack against whoever conquered them? Maybe disabled for players, dunno here.
    -Angles were actually really awesome and expanded naturally; as Colun and Went fought a bunch of wars they were able to attack them and take provinces. I suggest that the removal of the de jure casus belli for Colun or something similar to prevent them from declaring war early on the Angles and they SHOULD be able to pick up on their own from there; once they reach three provinces the only thing that stopped them from methodically and relatively slowly taking provinces was Dumnonia.
    -Western Britain split up really well. No complaints or much to say there, though will Irish invasions be added in? Dyfed and Lleyn split more because the lords there were able to form kingdoms; they're both briton.
    -Outside of the map, Ireland was under Midhe ascendancy but that collapsed recently. The Franks conquered like a third of the Roman governorships but since the system will obviously be changing (permanent alliances between all Roman governorships, maybe?) it's not as relevant. Scotland was unstable but no one really got a huge advantage, it was just a lot of back and forth between Pictavia, Cait, and some others.
    -Nicene Christianity spreads too quickly in Britain because of the moral authority given to it by the entire north being converted. I like the apostasy idea and would probably make a lot of sense, though just starting them off Brythonic would be easier, I suppose.

    No other real complaints. performance wise, game runs fine on 5 speed on observer, fine on 4 speed as a player, though I usually run 3 to avoid being overwhelmed.
