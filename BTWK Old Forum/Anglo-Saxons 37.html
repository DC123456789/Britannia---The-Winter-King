The chronology is largely based on that of the Iclingas which were claimed to have been settled in Middle Anglia by the 520s by Icel, who was the son of the last King of Angeln (who presumably led his people there).

    We need to give them the Norse coastal conquest cb, I imagine there will always be some coast from Isca Dumnonia to Manaw available in the period where the Jutes and Angles are still in their homeland. If not we can let them attack with a weaker cb. The actual abandon province decision would presumably just give them troops (the population of the province taking up arms and joining the army as a migrating tribe) and hand over the province without much fanfare - the cb should be persistent enough that you can always attack before, during, or after taking that decision.
  6. DC123456789 - 07-09-2015, 16:16
    I would do it through a decision, not a province decision, that migrates you entirely over to Britannia at once. Perhaps at the beginning you could first offer your "help" as foederati to existing Brythonic kingdoms, which is what most of them did during the 5th century - then once you're settled, you can proceed to rebel and wreck havoc. This is how basically all of the kingdoms on the coast (e.g. Kent, Essex, East Anglia, Meonware, Lindsey, Deira, Bernicia, Meonware, etc.) probably started. Later once there are already established kingdoms you could ask to be granted land on the frontier and expand from there like the Iclingas. It should be difficult to establish a lasting kingdom if you invade directly without going through the foederati phase or an alliance/suzerainty with existing powers, as that didn't really happen.
    Edit

  8. DC123456789 - 07-09-2015, 16:36
    I would probably have it so that most of it gets settled by Danes, but perhaps the liege would be able to keep a province or two. The province would also get a depopulated modifier that would prevent rulers from migrating if they don't have any non-depopulated provinces, so that you're not stuck in a cycle of your vassals constantly migrating if you really want to stay in Angeln.

    I can't think of any situations at the moment, but I suppose it might be useful somewhere.
