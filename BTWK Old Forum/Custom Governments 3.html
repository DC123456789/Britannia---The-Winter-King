  1. DC123456789 - 20-01-2016, 22:35
    I've done a bit of brainstorming about government types, and this is what I've come up with:

    Tribal: Represents the tribal societies of the Irish, Picts, Norse, "outer" Germanics, etc. Locked to the first three levels of tribal organization (which are also reformed to be harder to change/increase, decrease opinion more, etc.) and elective gavelkind. Will eventually reform to "High Tribal", which should take as long as reforming to feudal in vanilla. Severely reduced vassal and demesne limits, will tend toward tributaries for expansion.

    "High Tribal": Represents the tribal Brythonic kingdoms and the Germanic kingdoms settled in former Roman territory (i.e. Anglo-Saxons, Franks, eventually Alemanns, Rugii, etc.). Can build cities. Gain access to a higher "level" of tribal buildings (with increased gold, fort level, better troops), etc. Gains access to the top two levels of tribal authority, including gavelkind succession and raising levies/taxes with the very highest levels; possibly another level could be added for more nuance. Can reform to one of the feudal governments given certain triggers (i.e. culture, adjacent governments, etc.). Somewhat reduced vassal and demesne limits.

    Sub-Roman/Urban: Represents the Sub-Roman kingdoms of Soissons, the Visigoths, Burgundians, etc. Can hold cities (as well as castles). Can arbitrarily revoke duke and king titles as well as retract vassals. Somewhat higher vassal limits. Somewhat larger demesne limits(?). Vassals more likely to support higher crown authority//taxes/centralization. Doesn't use gavelkind (prefers primogeniture or feudal elective). Higher morale/organization for troops (i.e. proper Roman army). Depending on the circumstances, can eventually degrade to feudal. Can use viceroys if emperor tier (or maybe king tier as well?).

    Brythonic Sub-Roman/Urban: Represents the Sub-Roman kingdoms of Britannia and Armorica (Dumnonia, Gwent, Broceliande, etc.), very similar to the normal Sub-Roman. Can hold cities (as well as castles). Can arbitrarily revoke duke titles. Somewhat lower vassal limits. Vassals more likely to support higher crown authority//taxes/centralization. Doesn't use gavelkind (prefers primogeniture or feudal elective). Higher morale/organization for troops (i.e. proper Roman army). Depending on the circumstances, can eventually degrade to feudal.

    "Frankish": Represents the post-Gallic conquest/Christian conversion Frankish kingdoms. Can arbitrarily revoke duchy titles. Allows for concubines (the early Frankish monarchs were usually polygamous). Somewhat higher vassal limits. Somewhat larger demesne limits(?). Tolerates Sub-Roman vassals. Locked into gavelkind, and all the very bloody family feuds that it entails. Get "Frankish" mechanics, such as being highly regionalistic, appointing sub-kings and mayors of the palace, once we flesh them out. Allowed to revoke different religion titles? Can eventually degrade to feudal if crown authority lapses for too long.

    Feudal: The usual decentralized feudal government, basically something you collapse or are forced into.

    Bureaucratic/Imperial: For the Roman Empire or restored empires only. Not really sure what exactly to make this (that would be very different from Sub-Roman), but not a very high priority anyway.

    What do you think?

    The GoT mod has two playable Republic types - we could retool whatever they came up with.

    Some names we could consider could be taken from EUIV:
    - Tribal Confederation (or just Confederation/Federation)
    - Suburban (Sub-Urban)
    - Despotic (for proto-Feudal)
    - Administrative (possible descriptor for the first level of decay from Imperial)
    - Oligarchy (another possible descriptor for something with republican elements)
    - Bureaucratic (a high level of organisation, as you said)
    - Clan Council (or some variation on the word clan - the lowest level of organisation)
    - Vassalage (pre-feudalism?)
    - Aristocratic (a society decaying from Imperial to Feudal?)
    - Tributary (the lowest form perhaps?)
    - Autocratic (something highly organised, before decaying to feudalism - possibly Soissons)

    I also think we should have a few other governments:
    - Zealot or Fanatic (a simple rename of Holy Order, designed to be more contemporary)
    - Some kind of government type for mercenaries and possibly vassal foederati into which we can program levy bonuses and such
    - Keep Iqta in the game for whenever we add Muslims, double check that it's balanced
