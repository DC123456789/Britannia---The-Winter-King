  6. DC123456789 - 28-01-2016, 21:57
    @Isinfier
    Here. You can ignore cultures like Arabic and Jewish that aren't currently used ingame.

    @JasperClay
    Don't worry, balancing is probably the most important and time consuming task of all.
    Currently the Frankish invasions are quite railroaded in that the Franks get specific events to invade Soissons, Benoic, the Visigoths, and Burgundy, in turn, which should trigger around their historical/canon dates (486, 486, 507, and 534, respectively). The Franks when united under Clovis should be stronger than Soissons and should triumph most of the time (~80%?), and even if they don't win the invasions, they should still be generally be able to make progress against them via county or duchy conquests. Soissons gaining the upper hand should be rare; surviving is okay, but actually being really successful and creating the Imperium Galliarum should be a really rare occurrence. I'd say the Visigoths should be a little more powerful than Soissons, Burgundians about on par or a little less powerful but still normally be able to hold their own (this will be difficult to do because so much of their realm currently isn't on map, so if they're ahistorically weak that's okay for now). Even as a player Soissons should be a bit of a challenge. Visigoths should be fairly aggressive against Soissons, Burgundians less so (but they usually all go to war with each other fairly often so this shouldn't be an issue).

    Currently the only Alemanni on the map is a single county in Norfolk, all they need is a few dynasties so they don't all have the dynasty Smith.
    Smaland's rulers we know absolutely nothing about in this time period (unless you can find some mentions, even if only in myths/sagas or whatnot), so the plan is to make up rulers for them; each county should be independent with its own ruling dynasty (they'll be elevated to duchies soon to be more consistent, and some of the larger counties will be be split).
    Saxony is similar; the current king list is really just a composite list of various Saxon chiefs, most of which were probably unrelated to each other. In reality there was no united kingdom, but instead a multitude of chiefs or ealdormen that only united in times of war (a mechanic that I think we should implement later); I would say to demote the current kings to the dukes of Nordalbingia (the "heartland" of Saxony) and just make up rulers/dynasties for each of the other Saxon duchies, all of which should be independent.
