  7. JasperClay - 27-12-2015, 18:18
    Hey TWK people. I've finally gone from being a full-time student with two part times jobs to being a part-time student with one full-time job, and somehow, that alchemy has given me a sliver of spare time to actually mod, again. So, I've been play testing the Dev build, and I just wanted to send the following feedback.

    1.) England and Sussex, (486 especially)
    So, in 5 test games as Agricola and Arthur, the Saxons are barely a problem in the 486 scenario, even if I don't interfere to defend my lord. The biggest issue seems to be that Aelle dies pretty early on, and it just cripples his realm. I usually see Aengland fracture before 500, and then Gwent just begins to put the smack down on the Saxon remnants. This is especially easy for a player. I think Angle provinces need a slight buff. Maybe just start them with the same sort of high-population modifiers the Amoricans can get? There's almost certain a better, more dynamic way to do it with the new adventurer mechanics, or something.

    2.) British province strength rebalancing
    Gwent and Dumnonia are too strong. Siluria and Powys need a little buff, but I think some of the individual Gwent/Dumnonia provinces are simply too strong. I blame myself in part for that. I was doing a whole province-based rebalancing of buildings, but I gave up before I fixed that problem. Maybe it's good that I did: I think I would've made the Britons stronger, over all, even if I smoothed the differences between them, a bit. (Part of the issue is balancing the Glevum of the books with the archeological Glevum of the 5th century.) Do you guys think me looking at that again would be a good idea, or is there another way you're planning on "fixing" this issue?

    3.) Better Congenital Traits extensions
    So, right around when Horse Lords came out, I was alpha-testing a new "Epigenetics" system for BCT, wherein a character could improve or worsen on the basis of their stats in their teens. It's a way to allow parents/guardians to see a family/mentorship resemblance without giving the parents insanely awesome genes, or the guardians overpowered profession-specific traits. So, a 12 year old with quick and 16 learning might get bumped up to brilliant; on the other hand, a 17 year old with tough and 4 martial might drop down to hardy. I could never quite get it to work, though. Would anyone volunteer to take a look at it, after I edit it to fit into TWK? Also, I thought I saw that the duel engine had gotten a TWK overhaul, recently. Did that include working in BCT/TWK traits? If not, can I take a look at it? I actually made a BCT variant for the latest aGoT duel engine around July, but I never got the time to properly port it to TWK, I think.

    4.) 410 Characters
    I know it's still work in progress, but I'd like to propose a few character changes for 410/486, mainly because the current Ambrosius/Vortigen family trees bother me. If epigenetics works, both non-rulers and children can have their starting traits reduced.
    a.) Vortimer should probably be a more impressive character. Should he get a couple traits? Quick + Tall/Hardy?
    b.) I want to give Aurelius Ambrosius II Brilliant + Hardy, and his son Justinus, Quick. Justinus should also be married to someone with some ancestry and traits. I think a niece of Tewdric with Attractive + Hardy + Blood of MM, maybe?
    c.) I'd like to give Aurelius Ambrosius III Quick + Hardy (and blood of MM, maybe?)
    d.) In 410, I want to give Aurelius Ambrosius the Elder a wife with Brilliant, Tough, and possibly Tall. That way, we're likely to see kids as good as Aurelius II and Agricola. I'd also like to make her the sister/daughter of a British King, for roleplay/historical reasons. On the one hand, a noble British queen adds to Ambrosii legitimacy. On the other hand, if she's the sister of the King of Cantia, that gives Aurelius the Elder another compelling reason to worry about the Saxons: his wife's concern for her Western, coastal brother. Or, if she's from Pagenes, then that might explain how Agricola inherits Glouvia. I was also thinking she should be named Julia or Juliana (referencing the historical daughter of Agricola and wife of Tacitus). Maybe 17-19 years old in 410.

    5.) Ireland needs a story.
    I'm not exactly positive where TWK takes its Palladius/Patrick/Niall dates from, but I think it would be interesting to reconsider what ranges of dates for each seem plausible, and then pick the ones that give the most interesting 410/479 start. For example, I read somewhere the allegation that Niall might've actually reigned closer to 450, and that Patrick might've come even after that. Even partially accepting that chronology would move a lot of the more interesting chunks of late-Irish prehistory into the timeline, and create a nature framework for story events, without impacting any of the history we're more sure of.
  8. DC123456789 - 27-12-2015, 19:11
    1) Honestly, I wouldn't really recommend playing anything other than 479 for a truly balanced game. But yeah, Aelle dying too soon, Sussex being too weak, and Dumnonia being too strong are persistent problems that need to be addressed. What does Aelle usually die of, by the way?
    Do note as well that Cilternsaete and Middle Anglia falling after the death of Aelle is entirely WAD. I do agree though that pushing further should be difficult and require a lot of resources, though at this point I'm not quite sure how - a similar mechanic to the Armoricans would work, I suppose, with province modifiers that give high revolt risk and stronger revolts if you directly conquer it without leaving Anglo-Saxons in charge.

    2) I think part of the problem is that Dumnonia and Gwent are built up too much, in addition to the natural advantage of being feudal; if we simply remove some of the buildings in the history it would probably help. Perhaps nerfing feudal holdings a little bit relative to tribal holdings would also be good, which also has the advantage of buffing the Franks against Soissons as well. I haven't really noticed Gwent being overly powerful though, just Dumnonia.

    3) Sure, I could take a look (we haven't really touched BCT at all). The duel engine wasn't really modified except for adding in modifiers for combat rating; we didn't add in the new congenital traits but it would be very simply to do so go ahead.

    4)
    a) Vortimer actually doesn't have any scripted traits at all, if you want to flesh him out that's great.
    b) That makes sense for AAtY. Justinus is a madeup placeholder for a presumed generation between AAtY and Aurelius Caninus, so you can basically do whatever you want with traits. I'm more hesitant about adding in relationships or family members to historical characters that aren't historically or canonically founded, though; it's fine if you want to give Justinus a wife, but I would rather not add in a niece for Tewdric that is never mentioned as existing.
    c) You mean Aurelius Caninus? Quick and Hardy are fine, but see above for my take on his ancestry (and also note that all blood traits are intended to be agnatic).
    d) Adding a (young) Romano-British wife for AAtE is fine and probably historical (why would he have not left?), though note that AAtE's life is intended to be heavily railroaded, including the birth of AAtY (BTW, our intention is for the 410 start to go fully historical - which means no Agricola, etc.). I'm not sure that it would be appropriate for her to be a British princess, though; such a thing would definitely have been mentioned in the stories or as part of the ancestry of AAtY (since the Britons loved that sort of stuff). AAtE already has the king of Cantuaria as a tributary, so that already gives him ample reason to be concerned about it (and start a civil war over the Rowena affair). Our interpretation of how he gets Glouvia is that after one of the civil war, he was granted that area by Vortigern to appease him (which is also how he is intended to get it ingame) - it makes little sense for him to have family ties to Vortigern anyway given how his entire career was spent resisting Vortigern and his foederati.

    5) We're well aware of the "late" Niall chronology, but it's probably easier to leave Niall Noígíallach as just before the timeline to avoid having to create complex systems to replicate his conquests and all that it entailed, especially since the "aftermath" is generally considered when the real historical period of Ireland begins and generally resembles the rest of the time period much more closely. Ireland is mentioned in the books as being well on its way to conversion by 479, which pushes St. Patrick to the more traditional dates. Our plan was to have the 410 start cover St. Patrick's mission, with Christianity well established in Ireland by 479 (but by no means a done deal).

    2.) Sure. I'll go back to my old documents, and see what I can do. Gwent might just be a powerhouse for a player, but I feel like it was equal to Siluria, in the book.

    3.) I'll take at gander the duel engine, presently. I'll dropbox you a link for the epigenetics as soon as I fix any glaring errors.

    4.)
    a.) Will do. Make sure to let me know if I anyone is over/under done. It's kinda hard to subjectively balance from one perspective.
    b.) Okay, that makes sense to me. I suppose there's an interesting/complicating trade off when making up family for these guys, so I'll lay off Tewdric's extended relations. But it's unlikely AAtY's son marries a lowborn.
    c.) Oh, I see! I never even thought he was Aurelius Caninus. I'll keep the ancestry in mind; excellent.
    d.) Well, likewise, it doesn't make sense for him to stay if she's a British commoner; why does she care? I went with Cantia precisely because it wouldn't create any new alliances, but I'll just leave her without a family, for now. It'll probably make coding the events easier if she's got a charID #. I figured it's best to make her awesome so that AAtY and his siblings "look alike," trait-wise. (Also, that will make 'Agricola' possible, just unlikely.) It's a whole 'nother story if she'll be made barren afterward, or something.

    5.)
    It is definitely easier to do it with Niall offstage, and I totally understand why adding more work to the docket is not the most fun prospect. The reason why I suggested it is just because Ireland seems kind of lacking, now. Alba and Gaul have these engaging story lines of upheaval and invasion. Ireland has a slow religious conflict, and the ability to raid those areas. So, putting in something like:

    410 start: Niall, a few Christian counties, mostly Pelagian.
    ~430: Palladius, some Christianization, towards a 40/60 Christian/Pagan mix.
    ~450-479: Start of Patrick, 60/40 Christian/Pagan.

    Creates a similar 410 start for Ireland as for Alba and Gaul (410AD: Events that redraw political map). Then, you can have another parallel "479AD: The Last Stand of Paganism" narrative in Ireland. Patrick's mission either takes place before the start (~450), and vastly Christianizes it (75/25), or he arrives in (~475-80), and takes it from 60/40 Christian to 90/10. That also has the benefit of somewhat more historical accuracy than a 410 St. Patrick. If I recall correctly, Palladius almost definitely came before Patrick, and almost definitely landed ~430.

    And yeah, until Ireland gets those events, it will remain more boring than other areas. But that's no cause for stress, because it's not like having those characters with no special story events is more boring than the current set-up. (Unless there are already Irish story events that can't be moved easily, and I have no idea what I'm talking about. I just read a review on the new forum, and was thinking about it over my commute.)
  10. DC123456789 - 27-12-2015, 21:58
    1)
    a) There is such an event, but Arthur usually marries shortly after Uther dies and Guinevere often gets married off fairly quickly as well. I think if you lower their fertility by a lot the AI won't marry them, that could be an option.
    b) Aelle is already given much higher health than he should have, so I don't think we can really proceed more on that front. I guess the events for getting wounded, maimed, killed, etc. in battle could be modified to occur less often for Aelle.
    c) I feel like that doesn't really make much sense, based on history as well as the books any settled Anglo-Saxons (and Britons) should stay where they are once they get conquered. The issue (as with vanilla) is that it's far too easy to conquer and keep wrong-culture, wrong-religion provinces without giving them to a "local" ruler - that's kind of what I'm trying to emulate. In the books, Dumnonia could easily have destroyed all of Sussex but Sagramor refused to since keeping the Saxons they had already conquered under control was difficult enough.

    2) I don't remember exactly but I think Gwent was supposed to be stronger than Siluria, though not quite as much as Powys. I would be inclined to agree that Siluria is probably too weak, though.

    4) d) It is indeed an open question as to why AAtE bothered to stay in Britannia after the Roman withdrawal in the first place; it could be that he wanted to stay with his family, but we've also taken the interpretation that he was the last Roman-appointed governor of Maxima Caesariensis and may have stayed to support the Romano-British nobility and the Roman/Nicene cause in general.

    5) Obviously St. Patrick will not be there in 410, but the intention is for the event chain to only occur from a 410 start (as he's done, probably dead, by 479). Ireland will eventually get its own set of mechanics that will probably revolve around a lot of (cattle, because it's Ireland) raiding and fighting for the provincial kingships and the (titular) high kingship, with event chains for the high kings to try to assert their authority over all the other kings and try to truly unite the isle, like Brian Boru tried to. The issue with Ireland is that apart from the occasional county conquest it's supposed to remain fairly static, with no kingdom ever gaining supremacy for more than a generation or two, and the mechanics need to reflect that. Adding in Niall Noígíallach adds in a whole host of problems to such a system that I don't really think need to be addressed (same reason why we're not ever going to bother with a pre-410 start; it would be unnecessarily difficult to have to represent the Roman withdrawal from Britain, the Crossing of the Rhine, etc.
