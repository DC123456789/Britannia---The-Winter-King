  4. JasperClay - 28-12-2015, 13:38
    I'll look at the marriage events and see if I can alpha test some forbid-marriage traits unless someone else wants to. I might take a while.

    I think it's a little much, presently. It could happen half as often, and still not be uncommon. This is exacerbated by the AI clearly choosing its commanders on the basis of martial ability, with no regard for safety. Basically, a it makes a long reign by a high martial AI king impossible. Further, because it's governed by random event, and not the duel engine, it distorts the decision tree. My high martial, formidable fighter, strong, agile, tall King is equally likely to get killed as a dwarf, if he has brave. (Brave is a 1.5 modifier - there is none for martial, fighter traits, or BCT traits. Strong is a 0.7. Dwarf is 1.25.) So, in order to have a better chance of winning, I should keep him away from the front, to get the levy bonus. Smart, high-martial craven a make the best war leaders in this set up. Their high-martial will reduce the morale defense malus, and cravens are unlikely to die in the field.

    That might be historical, but it's going have the AI getting its best leaders killed regularly. I'll put in modifiers that account for BCT traits, martial, and fighter traits, but at that point, it's basically the duel engine, without the dramatic pop-ups. Hence, it makes sense to rationalize the events -and- shift to less random, more duel engine, rather than just one or the other.
  5. DC123456789 - 28-12-2015, 13:58
    Well, if you're going to be a great warrior king leading your army into battle all the time, you're going to be at high risk of getting killed or injured in battle at some point. But yes, it would make sense to add more trait modifiers to the death/injury in battle events.

    The duel engine represents an actual duel between two people going on (which is rarer), whereas the random battle events represent just getting killed or injured in the chaos of battle (which should be much more common and more unpredictable). They're two completely different things that should be handled by different events, even if being more successful at one also leads to being more successful at the other.
    Edit
  6. JasperClay - 28-12-2015, 15:00
    Sure. We agree. I think duels also capture one-on-one fights in the heat of combat well, but that's more medieval melee than Derfel's shield wall. So my mental images are probably anachronistic. And I might just be reacting to small sample size; I've gotten incapable Aelle's in at least two of ~8 runs. That just felt too high, especially as I definitely saw at least one other in which he was killed in battle, which may or may not have been the one in which he was maimed. (This is all before 500, starting in Enemy of God.)

    So, I'll first re-tune the battle events. I think the problem precisely is that they -are- predictable, at the moment. It's not that your great warrior King might die in battle, but that he will. I think my guiding principle will be that the cumulative risk of death in battle is logarithmic. So, an inexperienced commander is likely to have bad things happen; as is a poor warrior. But a warrior who's good enough to survive and become a veteran learns enough to live to fight another day. That will both allow for battle to be dangerous, but for truly exceptional leaders to parlay their skills into a long career. (Sort of like sports. Most players are in and out of the game in a couple seasons, but the truly great ones usually have long careers.)
