    I think Catalonia should be Romano-Hispanic, once we get there. Catalan arising as part of Occitan is probably more due to the shared Gothic heritage with Septimania, the influence of the Franks through the Spanish March, and a shared Mediterranean culture than being of the same Roman subculture.

  2. Ols - 02-01-2016, 13:15
    We definitely need it, but I'd like it to be at least somewhat faithful to the initial linguistic divisions in Latin, which didn't run neatly along the Alps or Pyrenees.

    This is a map of the Gallo-Romance languages:


    That's obviously a more modern span of it, but it can dip into Italy and Spain to some degree. Then there can be some full-on Roman culture in Central Italy, plus maybe Sardinian similar cultures elsewhere. If we ever add Dalmatia then a Dalmatian culture would be nice. There are I believe some extinct Romance languages from Raetia, which may warrant inclusion for an ahistorical resurgence scenario.

    The main reasoning behind this is that the presence of the Latin splits could be the trigger for melting-pot events. The presence of Romano-Gallic could cause division into French, some kind of Italian culture, Occitan, and Catalan depending on location. The presence of others could cause other cultures to emerge instead.
  3. DC123456789 - 02-01-2016, 14:19
    Well, Romansh and Ladin aren't extinct - those would be the descendants of Romano-Rhaetian.
    Romano-Gallic extending into Italy makes sense, I suppose, given the roots of Cisalpine Gaul, although maybe it could be split off into a separate culture.
    Dalmatia would be Romano-Illyrian if we ever add it (Dalmatian is just the medieval descendant that didn't get slavicized). Romano-Sardinian definitely makes sense as a separate Imperial culture.
    It should also be noted that Catalonia was Iberian, not Celtic, before the Roman conquest, so I'm still not sure how much it would make sense to make it Romano-Gallic.

    I also think that a Catalan culture is unnecessary, for the time period we're covering it was identical to Occitan.

  7. DC123456789 - 03-01-2016, 10:42
    I would say that geography had a lot to do with it - both areas were tied together quite tightly economically and culturally even before that, both of them having the distinct Mediterranean culture. The influence of the Spanish March probably helped as well, but I would say that it wouldn't be all that inconceivable for them to merge together even in its absence.

  8. JasperClay - 04-01-2016, 15:00
    It might make sense to go with Romano-Gallic up to the Ebro, and then imperiale Romano-Iberian along the rest of the Med coast, penetrating especially far inland in the south. The "interior" could then be a mix of cultures in the Celtic group (CeltIberian). You could differentiate the non-Indo-Europeans of the far west, too, but that split could also be modeled with Suebi local nobility.

    Key question:
    I'd suggest only putting Visgothic culture in duchy capitals or higher. I'd also suggest planning what melting pot outcomes we want before assigning cultures. Doing it the other way was part of what caused trouble for WtWSMS, I think.
  9. Ols - 04-01-2016, 17:02
    We could always keep it simple by having one-size-fits-all cultures first, and creating new ones later. Yes, we need to be careful if we want to set up melting pots.
  10. DC123456789 - 04-01-2016, 17:41
    There will definitely be Celtiberians (possibly split into Gallaecians, Astures, and Cantabrians) in the north and northwest as well as the Basques of course, but I think most of the interior and the rest of the coast fits as Romano-Hispanic (apart from the Visigothic and Suebi settlements, of course). I still think it makes sense to have Catalonia start off Romano-Hispanic (after all, it was in the province of Hispania and was originally inhabited by Iberian tribes) and merge into Occitan later (only if conquered by the Franks?).

    As for melting pot cultures, I was thinking of having Visigothic and Romano-Hispanic merge into some sort of "Romano-Gothic" or "Hispanic" culture in the 7th century or so, which would then break up into Galician, Leonese, Castillian, Aragonese, and Mozarabic/Andalusian upon the Arab conquest. Suebi would merge into Galician or assimilate into "Hispanic" if the Visigothic kingdom never falls, perhaps also with the option of the Gallaecian culture arising independently if the Suebi kingdom never falls and instead just gets progressively Romanized/Latinized (analogous to "Hispanic" for the Visigoths).
