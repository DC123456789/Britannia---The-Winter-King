  6. Ols - 16-01-2015, 21:16
    The Irish people and the Gaelic people are the same. I'm not even sure why we have a cultural distinction between the two anymore - it's basically just that Gaelic is a culture for realms under Ulaidh hegemony, whereas Irish is everyone else. Irish is the only Gaelic language in this period, so there was pretty much no solid demarcation between the two. Gaelic and Pictish on the other hand were almost certainly not mutally intelligible - although it's entirely possible that Pictish was still an Insular Celtic language.

    That's one of the websites I used - I'd prefer not to screw with the Gaelic/Irish mix much more unless we're planning on curtailing Gaelic. 407 is taken as the point at which Leinster completely lost control (one of the proposed Ui Niall chronologies) so you'll have to force Niall into the mod timeline itself in order to sort that out. Having him alive would be a pain in the arse, although a "Nine Hostages" bookmark would be interesting.

    Since I'm using a more mythical chronology that has some High Kings ruling earlier, the Dumnonii domination of the area would also fall before the timeline.

    I take this view on the Cruthin:
    Some scholars disagree, pointing out that although Cruthin was used to translate Picti into Irish, Picti was never used to translate the Old Irish term Cruthin into Latin.[9] Professor Dáibhí Ó Cróinín believes that the "notion that the Cruthin were 'Irish Picts' and were closely connected with the Picts of Scotland is quite mistaken"[10] and Professor Kenneth H. Jackson has said that the Cruthin "were not Picts, had no connection with the Picts, linguistic or otherwise, and are never called Picti by Irish writers".[11] The Cruthin cannot be distinguished by archaeology,[12] in historical times the Cruthin spoke Irish and followed the Irish derbfine system of inheritance rather than the matrilineal system sometimes attributed to the Picts of Britain.[9]
