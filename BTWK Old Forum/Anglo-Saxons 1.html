  10. DC123456789 - 15-11-2014, 19:22
    Okay, so this is kind of what I'm thinking about for the Anglo-Saxons:

    - ca. 500-510: Aelle dies, triggering a global narrative event. His heir inherits Anglia as usual, but through a special event chain most/all of his vassals will join the independence faction (unless he's a really good ruler) and revolt (probably getting some event troops as well) and will usually win. If they win, Anglia dissolves and splits up de jure as well (with East Seaxe and Mercia appearing and getting renamed to East Anglia, though Mercia should not be formable from Grentbryce alone). That way Anglia will break up most of the time without feeling too forced if a human plays it. With all the duchies independent, they'll certainly not be strong enough to advance against the Britons, but with their limited cbs the Britons probably won't advance too much either (unless Arthur becomes High King, which could be a problem). Hopefully Cantia will keep them in line. East Seaxe will probably get formed fairly soon by Aelle's heirs, with Grentbryce probably getting subjugated by Mercia later. East Anglia will probably stay disunited until the Anglo-Saxons can start using conquest against each other. Northampton will probably just get destroyed, which creates more room for Hwicce later. South Seaxe should probably revolt and form as well around this point, since it's a period of fragmentation for the Anglo-Saxons.

    - ca. 520: West Saxon invasion, landing on Portus Adurni. Over the next several years they slowly advance north and eventually conquer all of Hampshire (though that probably will and should take a while). They might need some help destroying Dumnonia later, though.

    - ca. 540-550: Iclingas (i.e. proto-Mercia) invade out of Grentbryce, eventually establishing themselves in Paegonsate and forming Mercia.

    - ca. 560: Anglo-Saxons in Ebrauc revolt and form Deira. Not long afterwards they'll probably get subjugated and become part of Northumbria, which is fine.

    - ca. 570: Hwicce invade Dobunnium (and perhaps parts of Baddon?) and establish another (petty) kingdom there. Eventually they'll probably get subjugated, likely by Mercia or perhaps West Seaxe.

    That should make the Anglo-Saxon invasions much more interesting with various different actors, as well as removing the large, ahistorical Anglia dominating everything.
