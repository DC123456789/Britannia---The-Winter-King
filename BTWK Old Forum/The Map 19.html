  6. BlackEagle78 - 15-11-2015, 07:53
    Hey, I saw the new version of the mod and I must say that the coloured-in provinces look pretty good in-game. However, I'm wondering whether it wouldn't be better to have the North Frisian islands be provinces of their own and not part of provinces of the mainland (not saying that all the islands should have their own provinces, but just that they shouldn't be part of mainland provinces). Fanø and Rømø could be together, Sylt could be on its own, just like Föhr, and the rest could be maintained.
    The way I designed it serves two purposes:
    1. The provinces were shaped in a way in which the provinces of Ribe and Tuner were still able to have ports, even though the North Frisian islands blocked most of the coastline; the small bay in between of Rømø and Föhr was intended to harbour the ports of these provinces.
    2. I thought up an event which simulates the Frisian migration to North Frisia in the 7th and 8th Centuries. It would trigger if, at any point, substential amounts of Frisia itself were occupied by a lord of another culture than Frisian (so e.g. when the duchies of Holland, Zeeland and Dorestad\Utrecht are owned by a Frank or Roman); the event text would describe that a large group of Frisian sought new lands for themselves and their people, which would be shown in-game by a spawned Frisian warlord with an event-spawned army, who would declare war upon the owner of the North Frisian isles (which at that moment are still called by another name). If the warlord succeeds, another event would fire, which would settle the region with Frisians and put the islands in the new, North Frisian duchy.
    I think this would be an interesting solution, seeing how it doesn't necessarily happens in every game, and how it would add unique flavour to the late game if it were to trigger then.

    Also, I want to add that Helgoland (Fositesland) was a possession of the Frisian king and also a Frisian religious sanctuary. I therefore propose that it becomes part of de jure East Frisia and becomes possession of the lords of East Frisia (and also gets Frisian culture).

    The Mofdings are mentioned in Widsith as fighting with the Myrgings, which means that are probably neighbours. The Myrgings are placed fairly firmly at the very south of Angeln as between Schleswig and the Eider (Fifeldor), and Schleswig is under the control of Freawine who is supposed to be a vassal of Offa, King of the Angles. This means that the Myrings are either Saxons across the Eider or Angles in the North Frisia area, and given that we needed an Angle name for North Frisia before it actually appears it seemed a good fit to put them there. Neither island was ever Frisian or considered part of North Frisia, though.
