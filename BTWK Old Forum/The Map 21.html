  7. DC123456789 - 20-11-2015, 16:29
    There's a great guide on the forum that I've linked to. Have you tried going on the new forum yet?

    Also, a few things to keep in mind:
    - All the provinces in Gaul/Belgica have been drawn with the Roman civitates/medieval diocese in mind (see the Roman de jure setup). Try to avoid messing this up as much as possible; adding new provinces is preferred to changing boundaries if this would result in messing up the Roman de jure structure. Flanders will have to be revised considerably with your new navigable rivers.
    - Try to overwrite as little of topology.bmp and colormap.dds as possible. I've noticed that you like to just blank out whole regions of land, including places you didn't touch directly; it's okay if you do this, but make sure to copy and paste the original onto it to maintain as much of the detail as possible.
    - Be careful when filling in provinces with the fill tool; make sure that the threshold/margin of error is zero. It looks like you accidentally overwrote part of the Thames river with the Litus Saxonicum in the last commit.
    Edit
