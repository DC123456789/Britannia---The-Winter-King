  1. JasperClay - 28-01-2016, 13:03
    Okay, so I'm breaking that long list of suggestions into individual posts, to make it manageable. This is one of them.

    So, as of my last play test, after the rebalancing, Dumnonia is still too strong. I did nerf the infrastructure in the kingdom marginally after that play test , but it might be a Demesne issue for Mordred. Last game, Mordred should have been able to shrug off the Angles, and did retake Cerdic's capital within a couple years. I think we should give Tone to Merlin's heir, Britannicus, or at least Hywel, his steward. Merlin could then be made a Duke, which is sensible, given his prestige. Besides, this way Mordred won't give Tone and Glastenning to some rando, and lose Merlin as a direct vassal.

    Likewise, I could probably restore Dumnonia's infrastructure if we gave Tone away and turned Sorviodunum over to somebody like Owain. Then, we let Mordred keep Vinocladia, and give Arthur Durnovaria and Mai Dun, when he returns. I especially like Owain for it, since we'll eventually have him and Arthur dueling - this makes the winner more obviously a powerful regent, and lets us give the loser's property away as it suits us.

    I said Dumnonia "should've" handled the Angles, as opposed to did handle them, because even though the Angles were outnumbered ~ 2500 to 4000, they still won, due to their alliance with Powys. Because Derfel's parentage is known, they get an alliance when he marries Ceweiyn. His father needs to be unknown, or set to some dummy character, to prevent this.

    The Angles also can't attack Dumnonia and Stronggore at the same time, presently. They're too weak. I'd propose two things.

    First, everytime any kind of Angle Invasion event fires, each German kingdom in Britain has the modifier "German Settlers" applied at random to 2-5 provinces in which the owner and all lieges are Germanic. This modifier gives the same bonuses as Brythonic Settlements, but it expires after 20 years. If it hits a provinces that isn't German, the culture flips when the modifier expires. If it fires on a province that has "recently conquered"/"transitionof rule," it is removed immediately, taking one of those province modifiers with it.

    Then, if that doesn't make the Angles sufficiently scary, I'll increase their starting infrastructure, and we can add vassal holy orders for them.

    That would work for settlers. I just wanted to apply modifiers that would be most helpful when we needed the Angles to be strong. So, even if Aelle is dead, I want the leftover duchies to get a boost when Northumbria puts pressure on the North, etc. Managing one large threat can be challenging, but it's usually either manageable or too big. That's the sort of thing we want to restrict to those dynamic mercenaries that mimic Foederati.
