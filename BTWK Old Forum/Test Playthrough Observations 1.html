  1. Frednutts - 23-03-2015, 19:22
    Ran a 410 test game this morning - Constantine III somehow managed to force a white peace with Honorious, and suffice to say, if that does happen ingame, there's no better start position than his - Gallic Empire roflstomped all of Britain, and Roman culture had basically covered the islands.

    Some notes though - is it realistic that Roman culture should be appearing in Britannia? In places like Londinium, I can sort of see the reasoning, our in-game Britannia was under Roman occupation for just under 400 years, yet not a single province is Roman culture, and only half the island is Romano-British. I don't really think it's realistic that Roman culture should spread easily in Britannia - in reality, the only culture changes should come from migrations like the Saxon/Frankish invasions, whereas Roman-style occupation should cause a Romanisation in the local culture - i.e. Briton -> Romano-British, instead of slowly painting the map purple.

    Also, Constantine's dynasty had over a 1000 members by the 8th century - needless to say the game was stuttering a tad...

  5. Frednutts - 29-07-2015, 13:33
    Ran a test game this afternoon, not expecting it to really work (given that I don't have a localisation folder), but it went suprisingly well.

    Starting in 410, both Cantia and Anglia had formed, with Cantia reaching its 479 boundaries, and Anglia being not too far off theirs.

    The Saxon plague did a good job of killing loads of Britons - I'll scrip an event that removes it from Saxons though, as a few of them were dying from it too.

    For some reason, almost every British character in southern Britain was dying from natural causes at a very young age - the province of MC changed hands at least 200 times in the span of fifty-odd years because the rulers all died within a month of inheriting the title. I though it might be because of the Saxon Plague, but the skulls had their deaths labelled as 'from natural causes', instead of 'from the Saxon Plague'.

    I think we need to restrict the ability for rulers of the Gallic Empire from being able to flip to Roman Culture and Hellenic Paganism (I think we should rename the religion from 'Roman' to 'Hellenic' because a) it wasn't exclusively Roman, and b) calling it Roman somehow legitimises it as the state religion of the WRE, when it fact it should be Christianity), as the Gallic Empire in this game (which survived Honorius' invasion even after Constantine was killed in battle by Constantius (which I thought was pretty cool) ended up both as Roman cultured and religion, when really it should at most be able to flip from Armorican to Romano-Gallic, and only flip to Roman religion under really weird/player controlled circumstances.

    I think I'll also add an event (barring any opposition) destroying the title of Hen Ogledd once the holder only has one kingdom title. Otherwise the title exists forever and ever. I could make up for it with a decision allowing Cumbric rulers with two kingdom level titles to easily reform Hen Ogledd if necessary.
