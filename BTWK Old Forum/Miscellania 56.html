    Some of these you may be fully aware of, or may be vanilla issues but i'll list them anyway...

    mercenary orders for briton pagans (i.e. vassals brotherhood of horsemen and britain) don't convert after reforming the pagan religion. if they hold land they always revolt, usually going landless during a revolt and becoming independent.
    no holy orders/holy orders don't convert - no reformed holy orders
    also, lack of holy orders for celtic pagans. I've got a few ideas for that i need to research, though.

    germanic culture has a higher frequency of strong/genius/attractive/etc, much higher than celtic - celts seem to only get 'bad' inherited traits.

    druids/warriors/etc leave court on succession? Constantly having to hire on succession gets extremely annoying when your reign is short lived.

    when converting province to pagan, it switches to the culture's version of paganism. That makes sense, but then the province flips to nicene almost immediately, as province has a different culture. Probably because of all those pesky nicene courtiers leaving on succession as well i'll bet.

    catholic spreads too quick - owned provinces you have turned reformed pagan can become catholic again in spread event. ai vassals hardly ever convert to reformed version of paganism, when you force conversion, usually there's a claimant lower vassals/dukes will revolt with who happens to be catholic.

    dumnonia starts out promigeniture, but can't change other king titles to promigeniture? btw, just never create king titles until you hit the tech for it. aren't romano-britons supposed to be able to change to that without the tech, anyway?

    i seem to only find the treasures of britannia when bankrupt, its less frequent finding them when i'm rich anyway

    (if you're playing romano-briton, educate your heirs to be briton instead, that way you still get to raid and grind down hwicce, mercia and wessex. that's if you've not retook cantia and icenia before the event then it's easy!)

    personal levies kept upon succession/inheritance - I had a fleet of 20 boats (eventually collecting 64 - way too OP when raiding, would be collecting 600-odd gold and prestige each trip)
    pagan bug - can declare war without dismissing fleet levies
    nicene bug - raiding fleet gets stuck at sea upon death if your successor is nicene and you die (until you dismiss them)

    Vikings invasion event - dena instantly took county of ebrauc without a war at event firing. they had previously bebbanburgh without me noticing. Not the county, it had been converted to feudal, then back to tribal by ai.
    they then decced a raid on me, but just darted about my lands and lost warscore.
    Same as what happens when you're whittling down the wessex/mercia troop spawns, abusing the ai is key with wessex and mercia. Hwicce can be mean though - they get warscore the fastest.
    (btw if you have retook anglia, the revolt spawn troops should be toughened up quite a bit. Usually a retinue can disperse of them quite easily without raising any county levies)

    my tactic with ck2 is to grant counties to temples instead of vassals. only do this if the county is pagan and same culture though otherwise you're asking for trouble.
    if i revoke the feudal/tribal county it still stays as a theocracy. usually holding the title on succession resets it back again (may be vanilla?)
    theocracies turn nicene when on christian land
    breton archdruid works fine (selection process) however gaelic becomes tanistry on reform? duchy-level landed theocracies are always tanistry until they run out of heirs as well
    when i revoked his title, the gaelic archdruidship went to another templeholder under a different reformed king, lost control of him but he was still the religious head - basically created a landless antipope with the authority hit included in the process.

    court bard needs an employ event like court druid. sidhes' can be expensive if your culture prevents you from raiding.

    dalriada has only one 1k troop spawn? it doesn’t reinforce, either - hence why they never conquer pictavia
    dalriada always ends up on rum with no vassals as a pagan when controlled by AI, but broken up and not able to dec war as they're mostly of the same dynasty.
    Eventually province flips to nicene and he converts. mainland argyll usually reconquested by pictish.
