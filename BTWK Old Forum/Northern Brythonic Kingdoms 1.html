  1. Frednutts - 13-11-2014, 20:37
    Some of my thoughts on northern Britain between the 410 and 479 dates:

    In the current titular history, Coel Hen is the High King of Hen Ogledd, and empire level title. Personally, I would have it as a titular kingdom level title, under a de jure High Kingdom of Britannia, though it's not important. His dominion encompasses the 479 de jure versions of the Kingdoms of Rheged, Elmet, and Cornovia, plus all the provinces in the 479 de facto Kingdom of Bryneich (which would place him on both sides of the current border of Hen Ogledd and Britannia. I would argue that his most suitable title would be 'King of Northern Britain'. He was also likely a Nicene Christian, and perhaps even Romano-British instead of Cumbric.
    The key strategic weakness of the kingdoms of Northern Britain in this period is that they insist on splitting up the land between the sons upon the rulers death, upon which, each son ruled as a 'king' over his new, smaller dominion. Upon Coel Hen's death in c.420, his realm is divided between his two sons;
    - Cerneu (already in the game) takes most of the realm, and retains the title 'King of Northern Britain'. He was later canonised for his stalwart defence against invading pagans (Picts, Scotti, and Angles), so too was likely a Nicene Christian.
    - Garbanion (not currently in the game) receives the north-eastern territories, namely the de jure duchies of Guotodin, Catraeth, and Rhufeing, and rules as 'King of Bryneich'

    The eventual demise of the northern kingdoms lies in this 'splitting up' of territory. I think a thorough look at and re-write of the family trees and who-rules-what will be needed in order to make this as historically accurate as possible. These 'kings' however still ruled more as 'Roman general' than 'Celtic king'. Whoever rules Ebrauc is treated as a first-among-equals of the kings of the north, and generally styles himself as 'King of Northern Britain'.

    By 450 (the arrival of the Saxons), the north should be split between the Kingdom of Bryneich (duchies of Guotodin, Catraeth, and Rhufeing), the Kingdom of Rheged (duchies of Llwyfenydd, Argoed), the Kingdom of Elmet (the eastern half of the duchy of The Peak), the Kingdom of the Peak (the western half of the duchy of The Peak), and the (High) Kingdom of Northern Britain (the duchy of Ebrauc). All the kings are cousins of various degrees, descended from Coel Hen.

    The Germanic peoples who settle in the north are mainly the Angles, and they remain loyal to their British rulers for much longer than the Saxons and Jutes in the south. The Angles were most likely settled in Deywr (the country of Petuaria, the later Angle Kingdom of Deira).
