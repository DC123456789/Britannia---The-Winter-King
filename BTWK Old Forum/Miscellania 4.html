    Well here's something I've never seen before. Left a test game running overnight:



    Looks like a relatively normal Briton/Saxon split island, right? Right?

    Wrong!



    Closer inspection reveals the tell-tale lines of cultural oppression. The Saxons may be Saxon (except for South Seaxe, which is Frisian), but the Brythonic Kingdoms are not Briton.



    They're Irish! Every single one of them is Irish! (And have been for a while.) When I left the game last night, the Saxons had chewed through parts of Dumnonia, and the first duchy of West Seaxe had formed. However, the Irish had conquered just a couple of provinces in SW Dumnonia, near Kernow. When I came back this morning, the entire western half of the island was under Irish rule - I've never seen them do anything other than eat a little bit of Wales from Demetia or Lleyn.

    Do any Brythonic Kingdoms survive? Well, if we zoom all the way in...



    Teeny, tiny, OPM Kingdom of Kernow, ruled by the same family (Llewellyn) all the way through, alone with its neighbour, the Lordship of Tregony, as the only Briton, Brythonic, independent realms left on the island.

    Of course, we know how much the Irish love the Nicene faith, so it's not surprising that Britannia now looks like this:



    Wow, right? Dem Irish! Never seen them be so aggressive before, so thought I'd share. May make us want to consider Irish de jure Kingdoms for Britannia (though obviously not a priority!).
