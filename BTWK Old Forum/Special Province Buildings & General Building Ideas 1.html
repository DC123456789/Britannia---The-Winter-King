  3. JasperClay - 21-02-2015, 07:15
    Okay, my apologies for not elaborating on this earlier, but I've been changing my plans significantly as I've been researching.

    DC - Yes, sort of. So, I was looking for some kind of archaeological review on the largest Sub-Roman settlements in Britain, but it looks like the scholarship is much more local than it is knitted together and macro. (Funnily enough, you guys might actually be doing legitimately new scholarship in this mod - I don't think anyone's made a map of Sub-Roman Britain this detailed, understandable given the guesswork involved.) So, after circling around it for a while, I just decided to research every location in Nennius's 28/33 cities of Britain. I tried to square historian's best guesses with the mod, and added a couple of places that were retrospectively important (like Edinburgh). Therefore, I'm prepared to assign buildings in this tree to 30-40 baronies. Theoretically, the buildings I'm proposing aren't location locked. That being said, it will be very unappealing to build them outside their historic locations.

    Now, obviously, I couldn't assign all of Nennius's cities the OP buildings I suggested above. I tried making different buildings ideas work a couple of ways, but trying to shoehorn Ft. Don and Letocetum into the same framework was annoying. I eventually just ranked the cities by military and economic importance, and then organically organized them into Tiers. So, with the 6 resulting tiers, I'd like to propose 12 or 18 new buildings, to be assigned to 35 baronies.

    But I'll stop here, to look for feedback. There's essentially 3 ways I can do this.

    The first is really cool and show-off-y, but possibly less fun for players. That is, I just name 35 unique buildings things like "Caer Brithon," "Caer Guorthigin," and "Caer Ebrauc," and have them do different things, depending on location. This would mostly be dynamic bonuses to garrison & levy size and local tax modifiers, so the province would only be as good as you made it. This would also be location locked. More important locations, like Caer Gloui and the ones I just mentioned, would get some static bonuses, too, like tax income and levy troops.

    The second is much more dynamic and formulaic. I make 12 buildings, 6 econ and 6 military, assign them in different combinations to various baronies. They're not location-locked, but the first building and the first couple upgrades take an insanely long time, and are insanely expensive. This is a nice choice, but not my favorite. I feel like, if I'm going to make generic buildings, they need to be a bit more interesting.

    My third option is my actual proposal. I make 18 buildings, in 6 tiers. A barony can only have 1 of the 18 buildings at a time, and there are trade-offs. Within each tier, a city can opt for the military-focused, economic-focused, or all-around versions of the building. (However, a military-focused Caer can't upgrade directly into an econ-focused one - it has to choose military or all-around. If it chooses all-around, its next upgrade can be to any one of the three.) The upper 3 tiers of this tree are flat-out awesome, and reasonably priced. The lower 3 tiers are way, way overpriced and not terribly awesome. So, having them is great, upgrading them is sometimes smart, and building them from scratch is an exercise in frustration.

    Whichever of these three suggestions we go with, this proposal goes hand-in-hand with two others. First, I'm making the values on the buildings with the assumption that levies will be reduced 20-33%. If they're reduced more, I need to do some trimming. Second, these buildings have to be made especially fragile. I'm thinking that if they're conquered by a different culture group than their owner, there's an 85-95% chance the building is destroyed, and a 15-5% chance the building is downgraded. If the barony they are in is simply occupied? I'd say a 45-50% chance the building is downgraded, and a 5-10% chance it's destroyed. (These chances could be lessened for the AI, but we will want the buildings in Colchester, Lincoln, London, and Kent to be destroyed, I think.)

    My next post will follow with some specifics.
