    I have a suggestion for the aftermath of the Battle of Camlann that would push the map back to the historical situation:

    Immediately following the battle:

    1) After Sagramor dies, authority over Eastern Dumnonia collapses
    2) Cerdic invades to Portus Adurni again, this time settling it comprehensively.
    3) The Middle Seaxe (under Cerdic) take Durocobrivis, torching it and enslaving the entire Briton population (becomes Anglo-Saxon). The Chilternsaete revolt (having remained in fealty to Dumnonia/Cynwidion and reform their petty kingdom.
    4) The warlords and brigands formerly employed by Mordred scatter to the East in the face of Meurig's forces and disavow his authority
    5) Celemion and Gwinttguic re-emerge following a military stalemate, being forced to convert to Christianity in the process (paganism extinguished in Dumnonia).

    In the years following the battle:

    6) Meurig rules Baddon, Avalon, half of "Cadarn" and Isca to his death.
    7) Meurig dies, Cadwy is acclaimed King of Dumnonia in Lindinis.
    8) Baddonicum reject Cadwy, remains part of Gwent (tied closely to Glevum).
    9) The Gwinttguic lose Clausentum to a Saxon landing around 518, which becomes the Ytene.
    10) Vectis falls to the Saxons on or just before 530.
