  1. BlackEagle78 - 16-01-2016, 16:04
    Recently, I've been reading up on the origins of Shakespeare's character of Hamlet. Apparently, the original model was the Jutish prince Amleþ, mentioned in several Middle Ages chronicles (Saxo Grammaticus, Gesta Danorum, Chronicon Lethrense). I therefore suggest that we model the transition of Jutland from Jutish to Danish in this way:
    At the 410 start, the Kingdom of Jutland is ruled by King Horwendill (called Harwendil in the Jutish name list, Hamlet Senior in the play). His brother Feng (Claudius) holds a duchy, while Horwendill's wife Geruþa (daughter of the Danish king, Gertrude in the play) has one son with the king: Amleþ (Hamlet Junior). By an event chain, Feng ends up killing Horwendill and usurps the throne (and marries Geruþa). We can then either have Amleþ be sent to Britain by event (to return to Jutland later), or just have the conclusion, where he duels Feng, happen directly. The duel event would have Amleþ, Feng, and Geruþa all die (or have Geruþa be killed anyways by accidental poisoning, but the other two by chance). If both duelists were to die (which I suggest giving a large in-game chance), the kingdom of Jutland would go to the Danish king (Geruþa's father), who would take the place of Prince Fortinbras in the play. This would be how Jutland comes under Danish rule.
    Another account (Finnesburg Fragment) mentions a large battle in Frisia, where some Jutes fought under Danish rule against the Frisians, taking place in 448. This means that the Amleþ event line should be before that date. The leader of the Jutes under Danish rule is Gefwolf, who I suggest to make a duke at the 410 start and also later under the Danish king. His son Gutholf and his grandson Garolf fought for king Finn of Frisia (so against the Danes and their (grand)father Gefwolf), and I suggest giving Gutholf a county in Frisia under the Frisian king (from the point where Jutland is ruled by the Danish in our files on). During the battle, Garolf would come to die, and there, the Gefwolfing dynasty would end; first, Gutholf would pass away, and a few years later, also Gefwolf. Gefwolf's duchy would then become ruled by a Dane. This is, of course, all only in our files; in-game, it could turn out to go differently.
    This is a period in which most about Jutland is unknown. By adding these characters and events, it would make the region both more interesting and it would model the Danish possession of Jutland in a semi-truthful way.

  3. DC123456789 - 16-01-2016, 19:34
    The only problem with that is that Saxo Grammaticus's Amleth is that he's supposed to be the grandson of Rørik Slyngebond, who is supposed to be King of Denmark (i.e. Zealand) in the 7th century (some generations after Hrólfr Kraki, who we have king in the 500s), so I don't know how much it would make sense it would make to push it all the way to the Danish conquest of Jutland.

    Gefwolf and his family are currently the pre-Danish kings (= dukes/princes) of Jutland - I suppose he could be made a tributary of the Danish king later, as Danes begin to settle in his kingdom and push the Jutes out.
    Giving Gutholf a Frisian county would make sense based on the stories. The only problem is that there is then a gap in the history between the last Jutish king and the first Danish king/prince of Denmark, but I suppose we could just shunt the timeframe of the Danish kings/princes back a little bit to fill it up - our date of 470 for the final turnover is probably too late anyway.
    Garulf is currently Gefwulf's son, but it would probably make sense to make him Gutholf/Guthulf's son.

    Also, just as an aside, Saxo Grammaticus was the person who wrote the Gesta Danorum, so it's kind of strange to cite them as two different sources.
