  4. Ols - 31-10-2015, 14:41
    Do we want it to be Bedouin or just generic 'Arab'? I've always wondered if it's reasonable to describe the rulers of al-Andalus as "Bedouin" when they're thoroughly disconnected from Arabia. The term used for them in the Qu'ran is also A'raab. I felt the term in vanilla was a way to distinguish between residents of the Arabian peninsula and the 'Levantines' and 'Egyptians' beside them. We may be better off having a monolithic Arab group but then a diverse Berber culture group including Numidian and others.

  6. Ols - 31-10-2015, 16:01
    Even if we have the mod end in the mid-10th century it does seem a little anachronistic. I suppose one could argue that the identity shift happened when the Caliphate of Cordoba was declared (just before the mod would end) or during the Fitna of al-Andalus (sometime after), but I wonder whether it's worth adding. Maghrebi Arabic might be worthwhile, but then again I'm not sure when North African royal courts became entirely Arab. Beforehand the two cultures should probably be separate. Doing this period is a refreshing opportunity to make the Berbers complex and fleshed out, and I wouldn't want to ruin that by having them be crippled by a melting pot during the Islamic phase of their history.
