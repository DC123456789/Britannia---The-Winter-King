  1. BlackEagle78 - 01-10-2015, 11:37
    Hey everyone! Thanks for adding me to the group. I hope I can add some nice and helpful things here.
    After playing your wonderful mod, I decided to re-write some of the name lists for ingame cultures. Afterwards, I wrote a private message on Reddit to Maswimelleu (who I presume is called Ols on this forum), telling him of my work. He was interested and I sent him this explanation:
    -The Franks: The old names included very latinized Frankish names, such as Clovis, Childeric and even Carl. I tried to Germanize these names, seeing as how the Christianization and latinization of the Franks doesn't necessarily happen every playthrough. Following this idea, the former three names change to the native Frankish Chlodowig, Childerik and Karl. Of course, I also changed many other names according to this idea; I basically replaced all lone c's with k's, but I chose to leave the ch's be. I also researched on the Internet for other Frankish names; monks, bishops, nobles, names from many different places. I also re-checked the existing names for more Germanic ways to write them and historical background.
    -The Vandals: The Vandals, living in Carthage and the former province of Africa at the beginning of the game's period, were already far in the process of latinization. I therefore used information and names from this source: http://www.academia.edu/1516556/THE_...OF_THE_VANDALS I also looked for other Vandal names (such as the regnal names), and changed them according to the rules in the article. I also added some names made of reconstructed words of the Vandal language, such as ''Blewbluma'' (Blue-Flower).
    -The Frisians: I checked the existing names for historical sources and also found a site with Old Frisian names. Although this language was sproken around 900-1300, the few historical sources of settlement of Frisia at the beginning of the timespan gave me a reason to use them. I also used some archaic names from a baby-names site (I only used names stamming from Old Frisian or Old German, seeing as how a possible Pagan Frisia wouldn't have many inhabitants with Hebrew-inspired names).
    The actual rewriting I did will follow in the next post.

  5. Ols - 01-10-2015, 12:09
    Here's our guide to getting linked to our SourceForge repository. We suggest using TortoiseSVN to do this, but it's up to you if you're more comfortable with a different piece of software.

    To do an SVN Checkout (i.e. get the mod for the first time):
    - Right click inside your\Documents\Paradox Interactive\Crusader Kings II folder
    - Click "SVN Checkout"
    - In "URL of Repository", enter "https://svn.code.sf.net/p/britanniathewinterking/svn/"
    - Click "OK". Your folder should start downloading (it'll take a while, since the whole mod is there)
    - Rename the folder to "mod" and put all your existing files and folders in your existing mod folder there. As long as you don't do anything with the other files with TortoiseSVN it'll ignore them.

    To do an SVN Update (i.e. check to see if anyone has made any changes and download them)
    - Right click the folder/file you want to update (in most cases you'll want to update the Britannia mod folder)
    - Click "SVN Update"
    - Wait for TortoiseSVN to finish looking for updates and download them.

    To do an SVN Commit (i.e. upload stuff to the mod)
    - Right click the folder/file you want to upload (in most cases you'll want to upload the Britannia mod folder)
    - Click "SVN Commit..."
    - At this point (or maybe when you're doing a checkout) it should prompt you to enter your sourceforge username and password. Do so.
    - You'll get a new window with an empty space at the top and listing all the things you've changed under it. The empty space is where you should put comments to tell everyone what you've changed that commit.
    - Click "OK"
    - Wait for TortoiseSVN to finish uploading your files. If there's a conflict with another file someone is uploading at the same time that it can't merge it'll tell you and stop the commit.

    To Show Log (i.e. look at all the changes that have ever been made to the SVN)
    - Right click the folder/file you want to see the log of (in most cases you'll want tosee the log of the Britannia mod folder)
    - Hover over "TortoiseSVN"
    - In the menu to the right, click "Show log"
    - You'll be able to see all the recent revisions, with their number (useful for identifying what version they are), the comments, and what files have changed. To see all the revisions ever that affected that folder, click "Show All" at the bottom left (it defaults to only shwoing the last 100 revisions)
    We also need you to have a SourceForge account to submit changes to our repository, which DC is better equipped to handle.
