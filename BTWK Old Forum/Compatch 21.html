  3. DC123456789 - 01-02-2015, 17:44
    By the way, do you think that it would make sense for culture conversion to also convert religions? For the most part the fprmerly Christian areas of Lloegyr did become pagan as the Anglo-Saxons settled there (in the book and historically), and I don't really see why any other culture change situation should be much different. Maybe there should be a restriction that you need to have at least one province of your religion for the religion to flip as well as culture, though.

  4. Ols - 01-02-2015, 17:49
    I'm somewhat reluctant because I've read that Hwicce remained Christian as the Saxons moved in. I think it may be better if it's a separate event driven by a variety of factors (such as proximity to the Saxon Shore). Cynwidion should become Germanic, but I think a pagan Saxon should find it harder and harder to eradicate Christianity as they advance. Gwent seems to be trying pretty damn hard to spread or maintain Christianity in Lloegyr in the books, so fighting Christianity should be a painful and punishing exercise. As a whole we need more events to permit rulers to persecute or marginalise Christianity in their realms, given that it can pop up at random.
  5. DC123456789 - 01-02-2015, 17:54
    Hwicce I'm fairly sure was an exception to the general rule (and this is reflected in their events), mixing with the Romano-British inhabitants (culturally and religiously) much more than in other areas. For the most part this would only affect Anglo-Saxons penetrating deeper into Britannia, anyway.
    Edit
  6. Ols - 01-02-2015, 17:56
    Is this something we should do right now? This may be better being linked into the religion overhaul project. I'd like a few days to consider our options at length so that we can create a fairly elaborate model for how Christianity can spread or be purged. An early baptism for the Saxons should be something that can happen in a minority of games, too. A lot of what happens could be linked to Gwent and its missionaries, as well as priorities set by Christian rulers in decisions/events. Given that it's difficult to think of a clear reform path for Saxons without Saxony, Jutland and Angeln being present on the map, I feel that they should have a sense of fervour which has some bearing on whether they take their belief system into Britannia along with their language and culture. It should be something that playable characters have a strong bearing on, rather than by railroading or pure chance.
  7. DC123456789 - 01-02-2015, 18:02
    It would be a simple change; the main reason is that the Romano-British areas tend to be basically all Nicene by the time the Anglo-Saxons invade it, which means that those areas will still be Nicene once they become Anglo-Saxon, whereas clearly at this early stage they became Germanic.
    Edit
  8. Ols - 01-02-2015, 18:10
    I know, but I'm concerned that it'd harm the conversion of the Saxons to Christianity by flipping provinces back to Germanic when they go Nicene. If most of Anglia was already Nicene (but the ruler was still Germanic) and something in Mercia flipped, it would seem strange. Actually making it credible would be harder than just Germanic ruler + Anglo-Saxon province = Germanic religion.
  9. DC123456789 - 01-02-2015, 18:16
    The Anglo-Saxons shouldn't be flipping to Nicene before the early to mid 6th century anyway. Ruler converting is problematic and inconsistent in general and something I definitely want to change for the next patch. Ideally I would have it require a percentage of your realm to be of your religion for that to happen, but that's something more complex to leave for later. I don't think that'll be much of a problem anyway until later in the game, but at that point the rulers should start converting as well. I would imagine that a ruler would try to make sure that most of the settlers would be of his religion if possible, since I imagine that culture change in these circumstances would at least partially be due to the efforts of the ruler.

    Anyway, other than this I'm satisfied and ready to release.
    Edit
  10. Ols - 01-02-2015, 18:18
    If you have a change you can quickly implement in this regard then do it, but it may be that it requires refinement very quickly.

    Getting religion right will probably be a pain because each religion should behave fairly differently but work in concert. If its working correctly, the Hwiccas should become Christian very quickly from their invasion.
