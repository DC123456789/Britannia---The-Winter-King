  3. JasperClay - 23-02-2015, 18:57
    Re:Random Birth - Yup. Random birth with traits occurs essentially in a manner that fills in the normal distribution. The {birth = X} in the trait line is the random births in 10K, iirc. So, between inheritance and random birth, approximately 15% of all births should have a (positive) rank 1 trait, something like 2.5-3% have a rank 2, and about 1% get a rank one. It's not perfectly stable - the number of "average" children will decline very gradually - but it's about as close as I could get it with the constraints of fun and CKII's lack of fractions.
